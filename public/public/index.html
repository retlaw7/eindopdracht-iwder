What is SSX 3?

SSX 3 is a snowboarding game from 2003. The game came out on the Ps2, Gamecube, X-Box and Gameboy Advance. The game has a Metacritic score of 93 and earned the award Must-Play. Over the years the game has aged, but the overall gameplay is still fun to pick up. You can do insane tricks with ease or go down the mountain at insane speeds to get to the finish and get that first place.

Gameplay

In the game you have 3 peaks, for each peak you have different events: race events and style events. In race events it's as simple as going down the mountain and not finishing last. The style events are all based around doing as many tricks as possible as you go down the mountain. You start at peak 1 and once you've done all events for one style of events you may continue to the next peak.

History

Before SSX 3 there were 2 other SSX titles, SSX and SSX Tricky. After SSX 3 there were SSX On Tour, SSX Blur and SSX (mainly referred to as SSX 2012).

The game has seen quite the changes throughout the years in gameplay, what started as a fun goofy game where you can do as many tricks as you want, in a world that has been shaped like only your wildest dreams could imagine, transformed into a more realistic less fantasized goofy kind of game. After the 2012 version, many people left the game completely and are still hoping on a return on one of the greatest games of all time. The speedrunning community is alive and well!

What makes this game so special?

What I find that makes the games so special is simply just the style aspect of the game. You can do the most insane tricks with flair and ease. The execution is so easy, it’ll feel like your doing barely anything but what you see happening on your screen is beyond your imagination. Jumping off your board doing a kickflip times a thousand while rotating your body like gravity doesn’t exist. It’s a one of a kind game, I’ve played this game so many times with my cousins and never felt bored. I got so attached to the game over the years, it just feels like home everytime I return. I still play the game occasionally going for the fastest times. It’s a must play if you haven’t given it a try yet.
